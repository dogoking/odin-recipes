Fufu and Njama Njama

DESCRIPTION

Turnip greens cooked like Njama Njama (huckleberry), a favorite from the Northwest region of Cameroon. Traditionally served with fufu corn and khati khati (spicy fire-grilled chicken in a tomato-based sauce).


INGREDIENTS

  • 4 bunches fresh turnip greens
  • 1/2 small onion sliced (julienne cut)
  • 2 scotch bonnet or habanero peppers
  • 3 tbsp canola oil
  • salt to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Thoroughly rinse turnip greens in cold water about 2-3 times. Remove large, tough veins and slice greens.
  2. Transfer into an ice bath. Once cool to touch, strain with a colander, and squeeze out as much water as you can. This yields about 3 cups of cooked greens. Set aside.
  3. Add onions and diced tomatoes into a large skillet or fry pan. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Toss in peppers, with the stalk still attached. Cook, stirring occasionally until tomatoes are reduced into a chunky sauce. About 10 minutes.
  4. Add in canola oil and fry for 3-5 minutes. Remove peppers and set aside. Season to taste (or slightly over) with salt.
  5. Add onions and diced tomatoes into a large skillet or fry pan. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Toss in peppers, with the stalk still attached. Cook, stirring occasionally until tomatoes are reduced into a chunky sauce. About 10 minutes.
  6. Stir in greens and cook for another 4-5 minutes or until desired tenderness of greens. Adjust seasoning if necessary.
  7. Serve with whole peppers.
